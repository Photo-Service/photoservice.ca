FAQ – Managing Your Photo Legacy

📷 Why should I organize my family photos now?
Because old photos deteriorate over time and digital files can easily get lost. Organizing your photos today preserves your memories and makes it easier to pass them on to future generations.
🗃️ What should I do with my old printed photos?
You can sort, restore, scan and file them in physical or digital albums. This protects them while making them easy to share with your family.
💾 How can I back up my photos so they're never lost?
By scanning them in high resolution, then backing them up on multiple formats: external hard drive, USB drive, cloud service, and as prints for important moments.
👵 What should I do with my parents' or grandparents' albums?
These albums often contain treasures. You can scan them page by page or extract photos for individual restoration, then create a new modern memory book.
📱 My photos are scattered across multiple devices… how do I sort it out?
Centralized organization is the key. You need to gather all photos in one place and sort them by date, event or person. That's exactly what we do as Quebec's only Certified Photo Manager.
🎁 Can a family photo project be given as a gift?
Absolutely. A memory album, tribute video or USB drive of family archives make unique and deeply meaningful gifts for birthdays, retirements or memorials.
🛑 What if my photos are already damaged?
It's often possible to digitally restore damaged photos: remove creases, stains, revive colours. Every memory deserves a second life.

Preparation Guide

Before starting, it's important to understand your motivation — your "why" behind this project.

Price List

Whether you want to create a legacy for future generations or simply bring order to your memories, follow these 5 phases to transform your collection into lasting digital archives. Liste des prix ▸

COLLECTION PHASE

Où commencez-vous? Vous devez rassembler toutes les photos, y compris celles oubliées dans de vieux appareils photo, disques durs externes, clés USB et ces vieilles photos sur CD/DVD.

Choisissez un endroit chez vous où vous avez de l'espace pour travailler. Mais gardez à l'esprit : dédiez cet endroit UNIQUEMENT à votre projet d'organisation de photos jusqu'à ce qu'il soit terminé.

SORTING PHASE

In this phase, it's helpful to think about how you'll want to access your photos in the future. Will you search by date, person or event?

Once you've decided how you'll most likely access your photos, follow the ABC of Photo Organization:

  • A is for Album — these photos deserve to be displayed in an album
  • B is for Box — to keep, archival quality
  • C is for Can — photos to discard
  • H is for History — does the photo tell a story?

SCANNING PHASE

In this phase, your goal is to decide how you will preserve your photos.

Will you scan physical photos with your phone? Buy a good scanner? Or have the work done by Photo Service for the best quality?

BACKUP PHASE

Once you've organized and digitized your photos, you now need to protect them for the future.

Decide whether you plan to keep your physical photos. Consider investing in archival-quality storage solutions.

In addition to the originals, have your memories on at least 2 of the following 3 options:

Clé USB

  • Stores your digitized memories
  • Easy viewing (TV, computer)
  • Ideal for sharing
  • Makes an excellent gift

DVD

  • Stores your digitized memories
  • Easy viewing (TV, computer)
  • Ideal for sharing
  • Makes an excellent gift

Téléchargement numérique

  • Convenient access to your files
  • Ideal for sharing with family
  • Download to computer or hard drive
  • Personal or family cloud options

ENJOY / SHARE / CELEBRATE PHASE

This phase is the reason you did all this work. Now it's time to enjoy all your photos — and it's easy now that they're organized!

For example, create captivating films and slideshows to relive the highlights of your past. Invite family and friends to a special viewing evening.
